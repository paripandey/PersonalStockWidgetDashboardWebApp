Glossary

By default, the trading session/period is considered to be "1 day" to represent the current weekday in question. However, we can also evaluate statistics over different time periods.

Term Definition
Opening price The price of the stock at the beginning of the trading session/period.
The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and the NASDAQ stock market open at 9:30 AM Eastern Time (ET) on weekdays.
Closing price The price of the stock at the end of the trading session/period.
The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and the NASDAQ stock market close at 4 PM Eastern Time (ET) on weekdays.
High price The stock's highest trading price during the given trading session/period.
Low price The stock's lowest trading price during the given trading session/period.
Number of transactions The number of transactions involving the stock during the given trading session/period.
Volume The number of shares that were traded (for a particular stock) in the trading session/period.
Volume weighted average price A measure of the average price of the stock, calculated by taking into account both the trading volume and the prices at which transactions occur. It gives more weight to prices with higher trading volume, providing a more representative average price.
